About Phombo Awajung Family

The Phombo Awajung Family belongs to the Limbu community, an indigenous group with deep-rooted traditions, history, and respect for ancestry. The Phombo clan represents unity, responsibility, and cultural continuity passed down through generations.

Within Limbu culture, it is traditionally believed that individuals from the same Phombo lineage should not marry each other. This practice preserves kinship respect, ancestral bonds, and social harmony within the community.

The Awajung lineage of the Phombo family gathers regularly to celebrate cultural programs, rituals, and community events. These gatherings are not only moments of joy but also opportunities to teach younger generations about identity, values, and heritage.

This website has been created as a digital home for the Phombo Awajung Family — a place to preserve memories, share photographs, document yearly programs, and strengthen our connection across locations and generations.

“Our culture lives when we remember, gather, and pass it forward.”
